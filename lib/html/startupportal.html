Elon Musk Invests $1.5M Into New Startup Called "Inventure"

Last Friday, Elon Musk publicly released a statement regarding his massive investment of $1.5 million into a new cutting edge startup called "Inventure". Founded by Nader Daruvala, Jaden Halevi, Nick Marji, and Gabe Veras in March of 2021, Inventure is a brand new startup out of the New York City Metro Area which serves to connect underrepresented startup founders with potential investors. "Inventure has amazing potential, and I'm happy to be taking part in its efforts to diversity the start up scene.", said Elon Musk in a statement realeased on Monday.

Bio & Contact

Elon Musk is a South African-born American entrepreneur and businessman who founded X.com in 1999 (which later became PayPal), SpaceX in 2002 and Tesla Motors in 2003. Musk became a multimillionaire in his late 20s when he sold his start-up company, Zip2, to a division of Compaq Computers. Musk made headlines in May 2012, when SpaceX launched a rocket that would send the first commercial vehicle to the International Space Station. He bolstered his portfolio with the purchase of SolarCity in 2016 and cemented his standing as a leader of industry by taking on an advisory role in the early days of President Donald Trump's administration.

Beffrey Preston Jezos is an American internet entrepreneur, industrialist, media proprietor, and investor. Jezos is the founder and CEO of the multi-national technology company Amazon. Catherine Duddy Wood (born November 26, 1955) is the founder, CEO, and CIO of ARK Investment Management LLC (Ark Invest), an investment management firm that managed the largest actively-managed exchange-traded fund in 2020.
